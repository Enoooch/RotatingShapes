How to do that?

To render everything i used Three.js, just like Mario Carillo in his amazing demo, go check it out! Three.js is the best and most popular library to do things with WebGL at the moment. And if you are just starting your 3D path, it is definitely the easiest way to get something working.

If you just rotate shape, its nothing special, its just rotating.

Interesting things start to happen when you rotate different parts of shape with different speeds. Let say i want top part of the object to rotate first, and than the rest, lets see whats going to happen.

To do this kind of things, you would need to use Vertex shader. I use a little bit of math and rotated parts of object depending on the Y or Z coordinate. In terms of simplified code it looks like this:

vec3 newposition = rotate(oldPosition, angle*Y_COORDINATE);

So with coordinate change, rotation would change as well. The rotate function itself includes a lot of Sine-Cosine's and matrices. All the math you need to rotate point around some axis.
